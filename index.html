Help Save My Family

Dear kind souls around the world,

My name is John, a 19-year-old from East Africa. I never thought I’d find myself in a position like this, but here I am—writing this with trembling hands and a heart heavy with sorrow. I hope someone, somewhere, will read this and offer a spark of hope in our darkest time.

Our Tragedy

Both of my parents—my mother Jessica and my father Francisco—are hospitalized, battling advanced stages of cancer. They are the most humble, loving parents anyone could wish for. But now they are lying in pain, fighting for their lives.

Doctors told us that treatment could prolong their lives, but it comes at a cost far beyond anything I can afford. I have already sold everything I owned—my clothes, my bed, even my phone—to try to pay for medication, scans, and transport to the hospital. I borrowed money from local lenders, but the debt grew too big. Because I couldn’t repay the loans, the bank took our only home. We are now homeless.

Our Daily Life

I sleep on the streets with my younger siblings. Sometimes, we go for days without eating. My younger brothers and sisters have dropped out of school, and I can’t look them in the eyes without feeling like I’ve failed them.

I have no job. I have no hope left—except you.

How You Can Help

  • Paying for my parents’ cancer treatment and medication
  • Renting a small place so we can sleep with dignity
  • Sending my siblings back to school
  • Buying food and covering basic survival needs
