Help Save My Family from Cancer and Homelessness

Dear kind souls around the world,

My name is John, a 19-year-old from East Africa. I never thought I'd find myself in a position like this, but here I am—writing this with trembling hands and a heart heavy with sorrow. I hope someone, somewhere, will read this and offer a spark of hope in our darkest time.

Our Tragedy

Both of my beloved parents—my mother Jessica and my father Francisco—are currently fighting for their lives in the hospital. They are battling late-stage cancer. Doctors say treatment could prolong their lives, but it comes with medical costs that are far beyond my ability to pay.

To save them, I have already sold everything I owned: my clothes, my bed, even my phone. I borrowed money from local lenders, but the interest kept growing. Eventually, the bank repossessed the only home we had left. We are now homeless.

Our Daily Reality

I now sleep on the streets with my younger siblings. Some nights, we have nothing to eat. They can no longer attend school. I see them cry, and I feel like I’ve failed them. I am jobless. I am desperate. But I still believe that people out there care.

  • Sending my siblings back to school
  • Buying food and other basic survival needs
